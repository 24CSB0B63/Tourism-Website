Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace
Built in 1791, this Indo-Islamic architectural gem in Bangalore was the summer retreat of Tipu Sultan, the "Tiger of Mysore." Known for its teakwood pillars, ornate frescoes, and intricate carvings, the palace houses a museum showcasing artifacts from Tipu’s reign, offering a glimpse into his legacy and Karnataka’s rich history.
